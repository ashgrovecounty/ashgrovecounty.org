For Immediate Release

Case #97-0441 Formally Reopened Following Archive Discovery

ASHGROVE, WY — The Ashgrove County Sheriff's Office announced today the formal reopening of Case #97-0441, a missing persons investigation originally filed October 14, 1997 and marked unresolved in March 1998.

The case was flagged for review after department staff, conducting routine cataloging as part of the ongoing archive digitization project, recovered a sealed evidence box associated with the original investigation. The box had been mislabeled and stored with unrelated records in an off-site facility. Contents of the box are currently under examination by Detective S. Dunmore and have not been disclosed publicly.

Case #97-0441 was one of five missing persons reports filed in Ashgrove County between October 1997 and March 1998. The original lead investigator, ████████████████, was himself reported missing in December 1997 while the cases were ongoing. That disappearance — logged as Case #97-0591 — was never resolved and remains open.

The public is encouraged to contact the ACSO with any information, however dated it may seem. Anonymous tips can be submitted via email at tips@ashgrovecounty.org .

Contact: R. Voss, Public Information Officer  |  (307) 362-1293 ext. 4
PR-2026-007 February 28, 2026  —  21:14 MST
For Immediate Release

Missing Person — Thomas R. Alderman, 34

ASHGROVE, WY — The Ashgrove County Sheriff's Office is requesting the public's assistance in locating Thomas Raymond Alderman, 34, of Ashgrove Township, Wyoming.

Mr. Alderman was last seen on the evening of Saturday, February 28, 2026, in the vicinity of Main Street and Route 12 in downtown Ashgrove. He was wearing a dark green canvas jacket, blue jeans, and brown work boots. He is described as 5'11", 185 lbs, with brown hair and brown eyes.

Mr. Alderman's vehicle — a 1998 dark blue Ford F-150, Wyoming plates WY-3841-K — has not been located. His employer reported him absent without contact on Monday, March 2. Family members state that Mr. Alderman had recently begun researching historical ACSO records and had spoken about "something he found" in the weeks prior to his disappearance.

Anyone with information on Mr. Alderman's whereabouts is urged to contact the ACSO immediately at (307) 362-1293.

PR-2026-003 January 3, 2026  —  08:00 MST

ACSO Launches Archive Digitization Initiative

ASHGROVE, WY — The Ashgrove County Sheriff's Office has commenced a multi-phase project to digitize, index, and archive departmental records dating back to 1971. The initiative, overseen by Records and Archives Coordinator B. Colton, is expected to run through late 2026.

Phase one covers patrol logs, incident reports, and associated evidence documentation from 1971–1990. Phase two will address the period 1990–2005, which includes case files of particular historical interest. Digitized materials cleared for public release will be made available through this website.

ACSO Records Room, lower level — archival materials currently under Phase 2 review
Contact: B. Colton, Records & Archives  |  (307) 362-1293 ext. 2
PR-2026-001 January 15, 2026  —  14:00 MST

Dana Hargrove Confirmed as 14th Sheriff of Ashgrove County

ASHGROVE, WY — Dana Hargrove has been formally sworn in as the 14th Sheriff of Ashgrove County, succeeding retiring Sheriff Robert Calloway, who served 22 years in the role. Sheriff Hargrove, who previously served as Undersheriff for six years, has 18 years total with the department. In a brief statement, Sheriff Hargrove cited the archive digitization initiative and "unfinished business in our own files" as top priorities for 2026.

// Department InfoAbout the Ashgrove County Sheriff's Office

The Ashgrove County Sheriff's Office was established in 1891, the same year Wyoming achieved statehood. For over 130 years, the department has served the residents of Ashgrove County with a commitment to integrity, professionalism, and public safety.

Our jurisdiction covers approximately 2,400 square miles of high plains and mountain terrain in central Wyoming. The ACSO operates as the primary law enforcement agency for all unincorporated areas of the county, working in close coordination with the Wyoming Highway Patrol, Wyoming DCI, and neighboring county agencies.

// HistoryDepartment Timeline
  • 1891
    Department founded by first Sheriff Elias Mossler following Wyoming statehood. Original staff: two deputies, one office on Ashgrove Township main road.
  • 1923
    New headquarters constructed on County Road 4. Building still in use today; renovated in 1967 and 2004.
  • 1958
    First patrol vehicles introduced. Prior to this, deputies patrolled on horseback or in personal vehicles.
  • 1974
    Dispatch center established in coordination with Ashgrove County Fire. 24-hour coverage began the following year.
  • 1997
    Series of unresolved disappearances — fall through winter. Five missing persons reports filed between October 1997 and March 1998. Lead investigator ████████████████ (Case #97-0591) disappeared December 9, 1997 while actively working the cases. Investigation stalled. All cases marked unresolved and archived in 1998. [ CASE #97-0441 REOPENED 2026 ]
  • 2004
    Sheriff R. Calloway elected. Served 22 years. Oversaw department growth from 8 to 14 sworn deputies.
  • 2026
    Sheriff D. Hargrove confirmed. Archive digitization project launched. Evidence box from 1997 investigation recovered. Case #97-0441 formally reopened, February 28: Thomas Alderman reported missing.
